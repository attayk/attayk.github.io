I work on and through the history of philosophy. My research is based in the conviction that the history of philosophy provides a central axis for examining philosophical perspectives, because it is constituted by an evolving, heterogeneous multiplicity of viewpoints. I am interested in expressing and explicating ideas regarding technique, value and knowledge by putting that heterogeneity to work. Systematically, my interests lie in the philosophy of science and technology, and in the theory of value. Historically, I focus on post-Kantian philosophy--with an emphasis on German Idealism and process philosophy.

I am currently a postdoctoral fellow at Potsdam Universität's Centre for Post-Kantian Philosophy under Prof. Thomas Khurana. As of 10/2026, I am a Lecturer at Tel Aviv University's Philosophy Faculty. Before these, I completed my doctoral work under the supervision of Prof. Eli Friedlander, and was a doctoral International Fox Fellow at Yale University under the supervision of Prof. Paul Franks. I also have a background in mathematical physics. I was a member of Prof. Larry Horwitz's mathematical physics seminar and published in the subject.
